<h2>Concussion Management Procedure</h2>
<p>To ensure the safety of student-athletes, and in accordance with Clark County School District (CCSD) and Nevada Interscholastic Activities Association (NIAA) policy, a student-athlete will not be allowed to return to participation in their sport until they have completed all of the steps in the <strong>Concussion Management Procedure</strong>. The Concussion Management Procedure is a multi-step process that includes the Return to Learn protocol followed by the Return to Play protocol.</p>
<p>Parent/Guardian and student-athlete must review the CCSD Head Injury/Possible Concussion: <a class="injury" data-file-name="26020_HeadInjury_PossibleConcussionManagement_08-01-24.pdf" data-resource-uuid="605d6ab4-f0d5-4f53-a908-ed599b78fb40" href="/fs/resource-manager/view/605d6ab4-f0d5-4f53-a908-ed599b78fb40" target="_blank"> Assessment and Management Policy</a>.</p>
<p>In order for the student-athlete to be eligible to Return to Play, they must complete all of the required steps below:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Step 1.</strong>The student-athlete must be evaluated and cleared to participate in the Return to Play protocol by a Licensed Healthcare Provider (LHCP), M.D. (Doctor of Medicine), D.O. (Doctor of Osteopathy), P.A. (Physician's Assistant), or N.P. (Nurse Practitioner).

	<ul>
<li>The school athletic trainer can provide the names of LHCPs specializing in concussion management, or the parent/guardian may choose one on their own.</li>
<li>The LHCPs release must be obtained in writing, specify the date the athlete can return to participation, and be turned in to the high school athletic trainer.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Step 2.</strong>The student-athlete must complete and pass standardized concussion testing (C3 Logix) administered by the athletic trainer.

	<ul>
<li>This test will help identify post-injury deficits the athlete can have with brain functions such as memory, vision, reaction time, and balance.</li>
<li>To determine pass or fail, test results will be compared to the student-athlete’s individual baseline or normative data if a baseline is not available.</li>
<li>The student-athlete will not be allowed to return to participation until a passing score is achieved.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><strong>Step 3.</strong>The student-athlete must complete a six-stage graded exercise program under the direct supervision of the high school’s Licensed Athletic Trainer.

	<ul>
<li>If, at any point in the program, the student-athlete demonstrates worsening symptoms, the athletic trainer will stop the activity progression, re-evaluate, and determine the best course in the recovery/return to participation process.</li>
<li><strong>The student-athlete must be symptom-free with all activities in order to pass the graded exercise phase and return to full participation</strong></li>
<li>The Graded Return to Play protocol consists of the following:</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>
<ul>
<li>
<ul style="list-style-type: lower-roman;">
<li>Symptom-limited activity [Up to 15 minutes]</li>
<li>Light aerobic exercise: 70% of age-predicted max HR [Up to 30 minutes]</li>
<li>Sport-specific exercise [Up to 60 minutes]</li>
<li>Full, non-contact practice; may begin progressive resistance training</li>
<li>Full contact practice</li>
<li>Return to sport; normal gameplay</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Any exacerbation of symptoms will prevent the student-athlete from progressing to the next stage, which will need to be repeated after 24 hours.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
