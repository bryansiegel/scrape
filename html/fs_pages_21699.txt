<h4>Kuldip Mangat, Coordinator</h4>
<p>A student may be excused from compulsory attendance at a public school when written evidence (Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool form) is provided to the Clark County School District (District) that a student will receive equivalent instruction. Upon receipt of the Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool form and the student's educational plan, the District will provide a receipt to the student's parent/guardian.</p>
<p>For more information about Nevada homeschool law, refer to <a contenteditable="false" href="http://leg.state.nv.us/NRS/NRS-392.html#NRS392Sec070" style="cursor: pointer;" target="_blank">Nevada Revised Statutes NRS 392.070</a>.</p>

<h4>Email: <br/>
<a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net" style="cursor: pointer;">CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net</a></h4>
<p><em>Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool</em> form and the student's educational plan must be submitted to your zoned school or via email: <a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net" style="cursor: pointer;">CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net</a>. Once the <em>Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool</em> form have been processed, the Clark County School District (District) will send the receipt to the parent/guardian listed mailing address via U.S. mail or via email.</p>
<p>For assistance, call 702-799-8630.    </p>
<p>Please note that the Education Services Division is the District's go-between for the required homeschool paperwork with the State of Nevada Department of Education.</p>
<p>Contact your child's zoned high school for qualifying testing dates. </p>
