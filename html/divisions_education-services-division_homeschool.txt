<h1>
	Office of Homeschool</h1>
<p class="addr-txt">

<hr/>
<h4>
	Kuldip Mangat, Coordinator</h4>
<p>
	A student may be excused from compulsory attendance at a public school when written evidence (Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool form) is provided to the Clark County School District (District) that a student will receive equivalent instruction. Upon receipt of the Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool form and the student's educational plan, the District will provide a receipt to the student's parent/guardian.</p>
<p>
	For more information about Nevada homeschool law, refer to <a href="http://leg.state.nv.us/NRS/NRS-392.html#NRS392Sec070" target="_blank">Nevada Revised Statutes NRS 392.070</a>.</p>

<h4>
	Email: <br>
<a href="mailto:CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net">CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net</a></br></h4>
<p>
<em>Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool</em> form and the student's educational plan must be submitted to your zoned school or via email: <a href="mailto:CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net">CCSDHomeschool@nv.ccsd.net</a>. Once the <em>Parent’s/Guardian’s Guide to Homeschool and the State of Nevada Notice of Intent to Homeschool</em> form have been processed, the Clark County School District (District) will send the receipt to the parent/guardian listed mailing address via U.S. mail or via email.</p>
<p>
	For assistance, call 702-799-8630.    </p>
<p>
	Please note that the Education Services Division is the District's go-between for the required homeschool paperwork with the State of Nevada Department of Education.</p>
<p>
	Contact your child's zoned high school for qualifying testing dates. </p>

<p>
	Upon returning to the District, please note the following regulations and state laws regarding grade-level placement:</p>
<ul>
<li>
		District <a href="https://ccsd.net/district/policies-regulations/pdf/5123_R.pdf" target="_blank">Regulation 5123</a> outlines the requirements for promotion, retention, and demotion of students, including credit requirements and criteria for promotion to high school on academic probation.</li>
<li>
		District <a href="https://ccsd.net/district/policies-regulations/notices/r-5111-age-of-entrance-an.pdf" target="_blank">Regulation 5111</a> requires students "shall be placed in the age-appropriate grade based upon their age as of August 1, unless retained or demoted in accordance with District policies and regulations. Age-appropriate grade is determined by the ages provided in NRS 392.040.”</li>
</ul>
