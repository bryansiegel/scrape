<h4>Clark County School District Police Department</h4>
<p>The Clark County School District (CCSD), the fifth-largest in the United States, encompasses nearly 8,000 square miles and serves the metropolitan Las Vegas area, surrounding communities, and rural regions. With more than 296,000 students enrolled across 374 schools, CCSD represents a diverse and expansive educational system.</p>

<div class="fsElement fsContent" data-use-new="true" id="fsEl_12104">
<div class="fsElementContent">
<div>
<p>Due to the district’s size and complexity, relying solely on traditional school resource officers—as done in many other jurisdictions—proved impractical. In response, CCSD established its own dedicated law enforcement agency: the Clark County School District Police Department (CCSDPD). This department is committed to fostering a safe, secure, and supportive environment for students, staff, and visitors.</p>
<p>The CCSDPD and it's officers are POST Category I certified by the State of Nevada, granting unrestricted law enforcement authority, including the ability to make arrests and issue traffic citations—comparable to other police agencies within the region.</p>
<p>The department is comprised of 200 sworn officers and 41 civilian employees. Its command structure includes 22 sergeants, six lieutenants, four captains, and a chief of police.</p>
<p>Operationally, the department is divided into three Area Commands. Each high school is assigned two officers, with additional patrol units covering all district zones, including rural communities such as Laughlin, Moapa, Sandy Valley, and Indian Springs. Officers are also responsible for supporting all elementary and middle schools within their respective areas.</p>
<p>CCSDPD maintains 24/7 patrol coverage of all District-owned properties to ensure continuous protection of students and staff.</p>
<p>In addition to routine patrols, the department operates a variety of specialized units and bureaus, including: Detectives, Training, Traffic, K-9, Diversion &amp; Wellness, Evidence, Records, Social Work Services, and a fully staffed 24/7 Dispatch Center.</p>

<div class="fsElement fsContent" data-use-new="true" id="fsEl_205922">
<div class="fsElementContent">
<p>The Clark County School District Police Department regards the investigation of all complaints as important to its overall mission. If you feel that you have a complaint against an employee of the Clark County School District Police Department, please contact the Department directly. The Internal Affairs Bureau will make every effort to ensure that the information provided to us is held in the strictest confidence.</p>
<p>CCSDPD will accept any form of complaint. Each complaint is taken very seriously and will always be fairly and impartially investigated. It is impossible to put a time limit on any investigation, as the seriousness of the allegations vary, but be assured that every effort will be made to update specific complainants about specific cases.</p>
<p><strong>As a reminder, this form is for complaints against school district police officers.  If you have a concern regarding a school district employee, please contact the appropriate site administrator.</strong></p>
<p>If you have a TRAFFIC COMPLAINT, please send those to: <a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:traffic@nv.ccsd.net">traffic@nv.ccsd.net</a></p>
<p><strong>The Internal Affairs Bureau can be reached anytime between the hours of 8:00 A.M. and 4:00 P.M., Monday through Friday at (702) 799-7830.</strong></p>

<div aria-hidden="true" class="fsElementContent">
<a id="fs-panel-205923"></a>
<div class="fsElement fsContent" data-use-new="true" id="fsEl_205924">
<div class="fsElementContent">
<p data-end="105" data-start="68">Police records are available to the public <strong>Monday through Friday</strong>, excluding holidays and Thursdays,<strong> between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 3:30 p.m.</strong> Records can be obtained at the Clark County School District Police Department, located at 120 Corporate Park Drive, Henderson, NV 89074.</p>
<p data-end="583" data-start="390">Individuals requesting records will be notified by the Records Unit regarding the availability of their report. A valid government-issued photo ID is required when retrieving records in person.</p>
<p data-end="956" data-start="585"><strong data-end="601" data-start="585">Please Note:</strong><br data-end="604" data-start="601"/>
Information involving juveniles is protected by law. If you are identified as a victim in an incident or crime report, you are entitled to receive a copy once the investigation has been completed. The Records Unit will process the necessary documentation and contact the requester once a determination has been made regarding the release of the report.</p>
<p data-end="1174" data-start="958">In accordance with department policy, only individuals named in a report—or their legal representatives—may be eligible to receive a copy. Witnesses are only permitted access to a copy of their own written statement.</p>
<p data-end="1174" data-start="958">If you have any questions or comments about the Records Unit, please call <a contenteditable="false" href="http://tel:7027997830;ext=5225/">(702) 799-7830 x5225</a>.</p>
