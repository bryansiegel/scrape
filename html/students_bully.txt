<h1>Say No To Bullying</h1>
<h2>CCSD Policy 5137: Safe and Respectful Learning Environment</h2>
<a href="http://ccsd.net/district/policies-regulations/pdf/5137_P.pdf" target="_blank">Policy 5137 (PDF)</a> -  Clark County School District is committed to providing a safe, secure, and respectful learning environment for all students and employees at all District facilities, school buildings, in school buses, on school grounds, and at school sponsored activities. The school district strives to address bullying and cyberbullying so that there is no disruption to the learning environment and learning process.
<h2>SafeVoice</h2>
<p>Parents/students are encouraged to report knowledge of bullying and/or cyberbullying, via <a href="https://www.p3tips.com/tipform.aspx?ID=5004#">SafeVoice</a> that allows individuals to anonymously report unlawful activities.</p>

			   <h3>KnowBullying App | SAMHSA</h3>

			   <h3>PACER Center’s National Bullying Prevention Center</h3>
PACER’s National Bullying Prevention Center was established in 2006. Complete with resources for students, parents, educators, and others, PACER recognizes bullying as a serious community topic that can impact education, the physical and emotional health, as well as the safety and well-being of students. The website includes videos, toolkits, posters, and handouts. 			  
			  <p class="bully-bottom"><a href="http://www.pacer.org/bullying/" target="_blank">Learn More</a> </p>
			  
			   <h3>Relationships between Bullying School Climate and Student Risk Behaviors | School Psychology</h3>

			  <p class="bully-bottom"><a href="http://psycnet.apa.org/?&fa=main.doiLanding&doi=10.1037/a0029350" target="">Learn More</a> </p>
