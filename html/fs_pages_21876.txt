<section class="fsElement fsPanel fsStyleAutoclear" data-use-new="true" id="fsEl_190743" role="tabpanel">
<header>
<h2 class="fsElementTitle"><a aria-controls="fsPanelContent_190743" aria-expanded="false" href="#fs-panel-190743" role="button">BER Equipment Replacement</a></h2>
</header>
<div aria-hidden="true" class="fsElementContent">
<a id="fs-panel-190743"></a>
<div class="fsElement fsContent" data-use-new="true" id="fsEl_190744">
<div class="fsElementContent">
<p>Funds may be available to assist schools in replacing equipment that has been determined to be beyond economical repair (BER). As first priority the District will replace BER equipment that is a safety issue.</p>
<ul>
<li>The equipment being replaced is within current standards.</li>
<li>The total value of the equipment being replaced is over $1,000.00 (multiple items can placed be on one BER request)</li>
<li>The replacement program uses a one-for-one type trade-in</li>
<li>For copiers, AIS will pick up only old Kyocera brand copiers when the replacement is delivered. For all other BER copiers and items or equipment deemed BER, the school or department must submit a <a contenteditable="false" href="https://prd-wd.ccsd.net:8121/sap/bc/webdynpro/sap/zmm_ship1_req?sap-client=400" style="cursor: pointer;" target="_blank">Shipping 1 form</a> to return the items to the warehouse to complete the replacement process. Please see <a contenteditable="false" data-file-name="how-to-use-the-shipping-1-form.pdf" data-resource-uuid="0785a2cd-cf2c-4a95-8e82-4b3f6718363f" href="/fs/resource-manager/view/0785a2cd-cf2c-4a95-8e82-4b3f6718363f" style="cursor: pointer;" target="_blank">How to use the SHIPPING-1 form</a> for instructions</li>
<li>The equipment has been deemed BER by CCSD Maintenance or the appropriate repair personnel and they have provided documentation of the reason for the BER</li>
<li>Additional required documentation: A school inventory of certain equipment. Please email <a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:BER@nv.ccsd.net" style="cursor: pointer;">BER@nv.ccsd.net</a><a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:ber@nv.ccsd.net" style="cursor: pointer;"> </a>for more information</li>
<li><strong>The Equipment Replacement Request Form will not be processed until all BER documentation is provided and attached</strong></li>
<li>The school agrees to pay a percentage of the cost toward the new equipment if the equipment has not met its useful life because of overuse or mishandling</li>
<li>The new equipment provided must be standard equipment</li>
</ul>
<p>The district will pay 100% of the replacement cost if the equipment has exceeded its useful life. If the equipment has not met its useful life, the school shall fund the remaining cost. Example: the useful life on most copiers is 7+ years. If the BER copier is older than 7 years, the district will replace it at no cost. If the copier is BER after 5 years the school will pay 28.5% and the district will pay 71.5%.</p>
<p>To request a BER replacement, please download and fill in the Equipment Replacement Request Form and email it to <a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:BER@nv.ccsd.net" style="cursor: pointer;">BER@nv.ccsd.net</a><a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:ber@nv.ccsd.net" style="cursor: pointer;"> </a>with the BER documentation and inventory attached.</p>

<h2>Non-Standard/Non-Supported Equipment Request Form (NSERF) and Process</h2>
<p>This District has begun a new process for purchasing Non-Standard/Non-Supported Equipment. The change includes the use of the Non-Standard/Non-Supported Equipment Request Form (NSERF).</p>
<p>Please note: There are many Standard items available in the on-line catalog. Please review the catalog to assure there is not a Standard item that will meet the functional requirements.</p>
<p>New form requires the requestor to complete and submit to the Operations Manager (OM)</p>
<ul>
<li>OM certifies that the building as is can support the item and that contractor work will not be required to install or operate equipment</li>
<li>Once approved by OM, requestor creates a Shopping Cart with the form attached as a PDF</li>
<li>Shopping Carts will be rejected back to end user if completed form is not attached</li>
</ul>
<p>Approvals will be through workflow in the SAP system</p>
<ul>
<li>Administrators will receive the Shopping Carts in their Approval in-box</li>
<li>Approvals will continue in the system</li>
<li>Rejections will be sent to end user with comments in Approval Notes</li>
</ul>
<p>Additional costs will be incurred with non-standard orders. The requestor acknowledges and understands:</p>
<ul>
<li>The District will not provide support for non-standard items. The cost of training, extended warranties, maintenance, or service contracts may be required and will be the responsibility of the requestor.</li>
<li>The District will not provide funding for supplies, accessories, or replacement units for non-standard items.</li>
<li>The facility and building infrastructure must be able to support the non-standard item. If modifications to the building are needed, please use the PAF process in lieu of this process.</li>
</ul>
<p>For any further questions, please contact Karen Purdy via <a contenteditable="false" href="mailto:purdykl@nv.ccsd.net?subject=NSERF" style="cursor: pointer;">email </a>or by calling (702) 799-5225.</p>

<h4>Minority, Women, Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (MWDBE)</h4>
<p class="p1">The Clark County School District is an integral part of the community. The District provides a foundation and focus for the future of our children. In addition to learning academic and employment skills, students must be prepared to work in a diverse, global marketplace. </p>
<p class="p1">Just as the District strives to have a diverse workforce in our schools, we seek the skills of a cross-section of supplier and contractors for goods and services. The District’s business relationships must be fair and open, and it is important that our workforce and business practices embrace all facets of the community. Opportunities to work with the District are open to all interested individuals and companies within the community. </p>
<p class="p1">Recognizing the talent and skills of our diverse community allows the District a broader perspective and larger competitive tool. The Clark County School District has instituted a “Supplier Outreach Program” to identify, encourage, and work with qualified suppliers --specifically, local, minority, women, and disadvantaged business enterprises. We hope this program will not only create a more competitive and cost effective environment by identifying additional potential suppliers, but also provide opportunities to strengthen our ties with the community and create partnerships with its diverse population. </p>
<p>We welcome you and look forward to working with you.</p>

<h2>Definitions</h2>
<h3>Minority Owned Business Enterprise (MBE)</h3>
<p>An independent and continuing Nevada business for profit which performs a commercially useful function and is at least 51% owned and controlled by one or more minority persons of Black American, Hispanic American, Asian-Pacific American or Native American.</p>
<h3>Women Owned Business Enterprise (WBE)</h3>
<p>An independent and continuing Nevada business for profit which performs a commercially useful function and is at least 51% owned and controlled by one or more women.</p>
<h3>Small Business Enterprise (SBE)</h3>
<p>An independent and continuing Nevada business for profit which performs a commercially useful function and is not owned and controlled by individuals designated as minority, women, or physically-challenged, and where gross annual sales does not exceed two million dollars ($2,000,000).</p>
<h3>Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)</h3>
<p>An independent and continuing Nevada business for profit which performs a commercially useful function and is owned and controlled by socially and economically disadvantaged individuals and where the average of the last three consecutive year's gross should total $17.4 million or less and personal net worth must be less than $750,000.</p>

<h2>
	Accomplishments 2004 - 2005</h2>
<p>
<strong>Participate in the Clark County Business Development "Meet the Public and Private Agencies Buyers and Purchasing Agents Forum".</strong></p>
<p>
	Three District employees representing both Purchasing and Construction Management attended the forum on June 16, 2005 and distributed brochures on how to do business with the District. Contact information, staff directories, and current bid information was also distributed.</p>
<p>
<strong>Purchase and staff a booth at the International Supply Management (ISM) Local Supplier Opportunity Faire.</strong></p>
<p>
	Eight Purchasing Department employees organized a CCSD Booth at the ISM Supplier Opportunity Faire held in March 22, 2005. Information regarding both Construction Management and Purchasing was distributed to over 2,000 suppliers.</p>
<p>
<strong>Collect and define spend data for reporting.</strong></p>
<p>
	Fiscal Year 04 spend data was collected and categorized for reporting. Reporting was split into two groups: Facilities purchases (FAC) and Purchasing (non-FAC) purchases. Spend reports were published on both the formally competed purchases and the informally competed purchases. As there are different statutes (NRS 338 vs NRS 332) with different dollar thresholds that govern these purchases, the reporting was as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>
		FAC Formally Competed Purchases (over $100,000)</li>
<li>
		FAC Informally Competed Purchases (under $100,000)</li>
<li>
		Purchasing Formally Competed Purchases (over $25,000)</li>
<li>
		Purchasing Informally Competed Purchases (under $25,000)</li>
</ul>
<p>
	Identified action item for next year: Significant percentage of suppliers have not completed District's supplier information page that asks for business type, size, and enterprise type, if applicable.</p>
<p>
	 </p>
<p>
<strong>Establish Supplier Outreach Committee, Goals &amp; Objectives.</strong></p>
<p>
	Supplier Outreach Committee was established within the Purchasing area. Facilities will designate representatives to participate as well as assist with funding if needed. Goals and Objectives were proposed for a 4-year period.</p>
<p>
<strong>"Doing Business with CCSD" webpage.</strong></p>
<p>
	Webpage "Doing Business with CCSD" was initially created in 2004. Website was updated and "went live" in September 2005.</p>
<p>
<strong>Offer training, outreach, and other tools to assist local and MWDBE firms.</strong></p>
<p>
	Negotiated free automatic notification and download services from Demandstar.com, the current internet hosting site for the Purchasing Department's competitive activities. Information on how to sign up was distributed at both the ISM Supplier Faire and the Clark County Forum.</p>
