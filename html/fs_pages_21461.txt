<h2>Pathway to Graduation for Students</h2>
<ol>
<li>Participate in academic planning meetings with a school counselor.</li>
<li>Enroll in coursework that will help to achieve your educational goals.</li>
<li>Parents/Guardians must review and approve the academic plan.</li>
<li>Take the ACT with Writing during junior year, at no cost. Students pursuing the Alternative Diploma will take the Nevada Alternate Assessment instead.<br/>
	 </li>
</ol>
<h2>Core Enrollment Expectations</h2>
<p>The Clark County School District (District) strives to prepare students for success in post-secondary education and in the workforce by providing a rigorous curriculum. The Core Enrollment Expectations are consistent with Nevada Revised Statutes (NRS) 389.018 and aligned with the State of Nevada  Millennium Scholarship minimum core curriculum requirements. To meet the requirements set forth by the Nevada State Board of Education, all students are enrolled in the Core Enrollment Expectations.</p>

<p>Students who successfully complete the Nevada Department of Education Core Enrollment Expectations outlined above with a grade point average (GPA) of at least 3.25 may qualify for the State of Nevada Millennium Scholarship. Please see your school counselor for more information about the state of Nevada Millennium Scholarship.</p>

<ol start="1" style="list-style-type: lower-alpha;">
<li>Students with significant cognitive disabilities may earn the Alternative Diploma through participation in the Nevada Alternate Assessment instead of the ACT. Students remain eligible for Individuals with Disabilities Education Act services until their 22nd birthday.<br/>
	 </li>
<li>Additional College and Career Ready Diploma requirements:</li>
</ol>
<ul style="list-style-type: circle; margin-left: 40px;">
<li>One mathematics credit must be Algebra II or higher, and</li>
<li>Proficiency in two languages, or two credits in: Advanced Placement courses, International Baccalaureate courses, Dual Enrollment/Dual Credit courses, a Career and Technical Education (CTE) program of study, Work-Based Learning courses, or a world language course, and</li>
<li>Earn a College-Ready endorsement (ACT in English of 18 or higher and in Math of 22 or higher) or a Career-Ready endorsement (CTE certification, OWINN certification, ASVAB of 50 or higher).</li>
</ul>
<ol start="3" style="list-style-type: lower-alpha;">
<li>One of the four units of mathematics must be Algebra II or higher.<br/>
	 </li>
<li>District students must take World History or Geography to earn either the Arts/Humanities/CTE credit for the Standard/Alternative Diploma through 2028 or the World History/Geography credit for the other diplomas.<br/>
	 </li>
<li>Flex Credits are: a second or third year CTE course, or a fourth year of mathematics (Algebra II or higher), or a third year of science, or a third year of social studies.</li>
</ol>
<p>A District-approved computer science course may count as either a fourth year of mathematics or a third year of science (one credit total) only after successful completion of the required mathematics or science coursework. (Senate Bill 200, 2017)</p>
<p>The maximum weighted GPA that can be earned is 4.950; this is achieved by earning a 4.0 unweighted GPA and 0.950 bonus points.<br/>
 </p>
