<h1>Safe and Respectful Learning Environments and Opportunities Division</h1>
<p>The Safe and Respectful Learning Environments and Opportunities (SRLEO) Division envisions a Clark County School District where everyone thrives: students, educators, families, and community members. We strive for vibrant, inclusive, and affirming educational environments. We foster these environments through student-centered leadership and culturally sustaining practices. Our approach creates positive, proactive learning spaces that enhance academic, social-emotional, and behavioral success for all learners.</p>
<p>We are deeply committed to ensuring high expectations, support, fairness, access, and respect for every student, family, and staff member. By championing inclusive policies, supportive practices, and responsive instruction, we construct school communities where every student unlocks their full potential. We empower educators with high-quality tools and impactful professional learning to drive systemic improvement. This strengthens our educators' capacity to meet each student's unique needs, resulting in improved academic achievement and positive behavioral outcomes.</p>
<p>By aligning our practices with district policies and student-centered responsive principles, SRLEO promotes academic success, cultivates strong relationships with families and communities, and upholds legal and ethical standards.</p>

            <h2>Achieving Equity and Access in the Clark County School District</h2>

            <p>In July 2019, Superintendent Jesus F. Jara created the Student Equity and Access Advisory Commission. The
                Commission comprised of 13 leaders throughout the community with diverse backgrounds, motivated to
                ensure the success of all students. Its charge was to make recommendations to the Superintendent to
                ensure equity and access for all students.</p>

            <p>The Commission participated in regular meetings and discussions over six months to accomplish its charge.
                During that time, the Commission reviewed extensive school-by-school data to better understand the
                current status of academic programming across our District and the critical areas in a student’s K-12
                journey wherein our system has created opportunities and access for some and prevalent hurdles and
                barriers for others.</p>
